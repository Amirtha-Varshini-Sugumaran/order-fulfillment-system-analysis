# AS-IS / TO-BE Process Analysis

## Current AS-IS Process

Orders are currently received by email or phone and entered into an Excel tracker. Operations staff manually check delivery requirements, email or call drivers, and update the spreadsheet when they receive information back.

Customer support does not always have the latest status. When a customer calls, support may need to check the spreadsheet, search email threads, or contact operations.

Delays are often handled reactively. A delivery may only be flagged as delayed after a customer calls or a driver reports an issue.

## Future TO-BE Process

Orders will be created in a central system. Operations staff will assign deliveries to drivers and update delivery plans in one place. Drivers will update delivery status directly, and customer support will be able to search the system for the latest status.

Managers will have a simple dashboard showing active work, delays, and completed deliveries.

The process will still allow human judgement, but the tracking will no longer depend on spreadsheets and email chains.

## Comparison Table

| Area | AS-IS | TO-BE |
|---|---|---|
| Order intake | Orders recorded in Excel after email or phone request | Orders created directly in the system |
| Delivery assignment | Driver assignment handled by email or phone | Driver selected and assigned in the system |
| Status tracking | Spreadsheet updated manually when someone receives an update | Status updated in the system with timestamp |
| Customer queries | Support checks Excel or asks operations | Support searches order and sees latest status |
| Delay visibility | Delays often noticed late | Delayed orders flagged on dashboard |
| Reporting | Manual counts from spreadsheet | Basic dashboard KPIs |
| Audit trail | Limited and spread across emails | Status history stored against each order |
| Driver workload | Managed through local knowledge | Active orders per driver visible |
| Data quality | Inconsistent fields and manual notes | Required fields and controlled status values |
